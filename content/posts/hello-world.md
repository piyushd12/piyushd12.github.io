For a long time, I treated writing as something other people did — authors, academics, and tech influencers with polished social media presences. I was the one quietly reading their posts at midnight, learning things, but never contributing back. This blog is me deciding to change that.

The tipping point was a particularly gnarly debugging session at work. I spent three days chasing a race condition in a Go service that only manifested under load. When I finally cracked it, I had this urge to write it down — not just in a Notion doc for myself, but somewhere public, somewhere that might save the next person from the same three-day rabbit hole. That post is coming. This one is just the introduction.

I plan to write about the things I genuinely spend time on: backend systems in Go and Python, containerisation and orchestration, database internals, and the craft of writing clean, maintainable code. I won't pretend to be an expert on everything — I'll write about things I'm actively learning just as often as things I already know well. If even one post saves you an hour, this will have been worth it. Welcome, and thanks for reading.
